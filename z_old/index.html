TABUU 2013

MOBILE DEVELOPMENT · UX/UI DESIGN
Tabuu is a game of guessing and wordplay, and requires teammates to guess each other’s card using a list of words the ‘guessee’ can not use to describe the intended word.

A modern take on the classic card game

For this update to an early Android app made by myself and a partner, I decided to redesign the app from its current flat, static appearance, and incorporate many animating elements that make the displayed cards feel more tangible. The start menu was drastically overhauled to be more of a landing page than anything else, and so what was before a simple list of modifiers is now reduced to “Quick Play” and “Game Setup.” The original app had selectable ‘themed’ decks, and so with this update I recreated the deck selector to be primarily visual, showcasing actual decks as opposed to simply text.

Reworked for gesture-based gameplay

The cards themselves haven’t changed too much in appearance, getting a minor facelift to give them a more realistic look. They do however have new animations that give them more contextual controls, with finger swiping directions relaying whether a team correctly guesses a card, or if the card is skipped. These controls make the gameplay extremely intuitive, and in the event the two swipes are not clear, a small question mark can be pressed to display on-screen directions. This new Tabuu marks a huge step in the apps creation, and also serves to show just how far we’ve come from our humble beginnings.

OLYMPIC GAMES 2012

MOBILE DESIGN · SOCIAL INTEGRATION
This iPad application allows fans of the Olympic Games to attempt recreations of iconic Olympian photos and enter them into a competition to win a trip to the 2012 London Olympic Games.

Engaging Olympic fans in a new way

The International Olympic Committee, or IOC, had used Mass Relevance a year prior for the Youth Olympic Games, and approached us again to create another social-based app for the “I Copy U” competition they were holding for the 2012 Summer Olympic Games in London. I was tasked with coming up with the visual design of the app, and discussions regarding the app’s functionality began with representative from the IOC. After a couple weeks of brainstorming and some back-and-forth, the IOC and those of us working on this project felt comfortable enough to move forward with designing and building the app.

Giving depth to the depthless

To give the app a bit more visual fidelity I incorporated an image element to give the background a bit more depth, which it lacked due to the flat elements required by the branding guidelines. We had used a more sporty typeface for our last IOC app, and for this we decided to go with something less rigid and more plain, so in the end we used Helvetica Neue. After finishing up the design, a team of developers built the app, and it was a huge success upon it’s release, exceeding the IOC’s expectations in generating social content and presence.

URBAN SPRAWL 2012

GAME DESIGN · MOBILE DEVELOPMENT
The town of Jarlsberg is booming. Residents are flocking and buying up plots of land at a record pace, and it’s up to you, the Mayor, to give these residents the amenities they need in order for Jarlsberg to stay afloat and ensure you don’t go bankrupt.

The mechanics of an ever-growing city

Urban Sprawl began life as a Ludum Dare entry, a contest in which teams of game developers are tasked with creating a game from scratch in 72-hours or less based on a specific theme, in this case being “Evolution.” My team, The Gaming Project, landed on the idea of an evolving city, and began to map out the specific mechanics that would be involved in gameplay. While our lead programmer started work on the mechanics, I came up with a pixel-art style utilizing a palette of reds, greens, and blues for each of the structures in order to visually differentiate them.

Things get a bit more pixel-y

After the Dare had ended, we knew we had to bring Urban Sprawl to mobile, and so we started development of Android and iOS versions of the game. I knew that while the current artwork for the game held up for a game made in 72-hours, it would have to be updated to meet the quality users come to expect in the finest mobile titles. I started by upsizing the current structure assets to include more detail, as well as modifying the current buildings to closely match their real-world appearances. For the UI, I kept it simple, relying more on contextual pop-ups and notifiers.

CALLAWAY 2012

WEB DESIGN · SOCIAL INTEGRATION
This was a ‘Flock-to-Unlock’ landing page where viewers could Tweet to help unlock exclusive videos by golf pros and celebrities such as Phil Mickelson and Justin Timberlake.

Fans team up to unlock content

Callaway wanted to leverage social content for the upcoming US Open, in which Phil Mickelson would be their star player, and so I provided one of Mass Relevance's newer social concepts for them, in which users flocked to a minisite on Callaway's webpage to collectively mention the term "#ADayWithPhil" in their user-generated content in order to unlock a video showing Phil preparing for his US Open appearance. The addition of an avatar wall helped reinforce the group effort required to unlock the content.

A stroke of ingenuity

In addition to the Day With Phil page, I also created a concept for one of Callaway's other sponsored golfers, singer and actor Justin Timberlake, in which users could come and ask Justin questions which he would respond to over Twitter. While these Tweets would not appear compelling on Twitter's site, this microsite clearly organized and gathered the questions and answers to make a visually compelling experience for viewers.

WWE 2012

MULTIMEDIA DESIGN · CROSS-PLATFORM INTEGRATION
These concepts helped the World Wrestling Entertainment visualize how social content could be use to augment and engage audiences for their new 3-hour weekly events.

The fans control the story

With the advent of World Wresting Entertainment's new three-hour long Monday Night Raw program, the WWE wanted to leverage and incorporate social content online and on the television show in order to engage users for the long period of time they would be watching the show, in an attempt to further entertain their home viewership. They approached Mass Relevance and I was tasked with delivering several potential concepts for Twitter content displays.

"It's not sports. It's entertainment."

The WWE prides itself on being a prime source of entertainment, with the various side-stories being one of the main highlights of the entertainment provider. I came up with the idea of utilizing Twitter content to help augment these storylines, with fans voting on major plotpoints in order to feel more deeply connected with the storylines. To help make the results feel more personal for the viewers, I added a Twitter 'heatmap' on the WWE microsite to give further context to the gathered votes.

MASS RELEVANCE INFOGRAPHS 2011

INFORMATION DESIGN · DATA PRESENTATION
This collection of infographics helped Mass Relevance promote and explain their social aggregation platform as well as gather pertinent social research data.

Giving visual context to ones and zeroes

The Mass Relevance platform provides an extremely easy way for users to gather and resyndicate social content from a variety of social networks, however for the average joe, the technical details might seem mystifying, and so I created an infographic clearly explaining the 'journey' of social content, from the source to the user's display. However, this was merely one side of the infographics I helped create for Mass Relevance.

From technical jargon to layman speak

In addition to the more technical infographs I helped create for Mass Relevance, I also made infographs gathering data on Social TV viewership which required me to research the then unknown space and create a compelling narrative that involved personifying the distinct groups of Social TV users which helped give the bland data more meaningful context.

ICON COLLAGES 2011

ART & LAYOUT · PRINT SERIES
These designs, printed on a variety of media, are iconic symbols from Half-Life 2, The Legend of Zelda, and Minecraft, all made up of hundreds of small icons from each game.

An homage to iconic items

Being a huge fan of gaming, I decided to create homages to some of my favorite titles. Each work is a collage of several in-game items used by the player, from the tools of Minecraft to the various Master Swords weilded by Link in the various Zelda titles. After several hours of laying out the collages themselves, carefully placing the items in a visually appealing way, I chose to cover the finished pieces in a two-color gradient, each related closely the the game the collage was based off of.
